    Aftercare Program

    Addiction isn’t a condition that just ends when people finish rehab. It’s a disorder that they continue to battle long afterward. Thankfully, aftercare treatment can provide ongoing care. However, what exactly is this treatment, and how does it benefit those who struggle with addiction?


    WHAT IS AFTERCARE?

    Aftercare, also known as continued treatment or ongoing care, is a specific program that occurs directly after rehab. In fact, before people enroll in continued care, they complete an initial addiction rehab program. Most of the time, this prior treatment is a residential program. However, it could be varying levels of outpatient rehab. During an aftercare program, they have access to many of the same services that they did during rehab. With that said, the actual programs that they participate in vary depending on their individual needs. In general, ongoing care involves different types of therapies and support groups. The goal is to help people transition from rehab to living on their own. Making a smooth transition is essential in helping them avoid relapse. As helpful as these types of programs are, it’s just as important to complete other types of rehab. Continued treatment builds on the goals and skills that people learned during rehab. Without a solid foundation, aftercare doesn’t work.

    IS A CONTINUED TREATMENT PROGRAM REALLY NECESSARY?

    It’s vital that people don’t underestimate the importance of an aftercare program. It’s often simple for them to sweep it under the rug and forget about it. They generally do because they feel good after rehab and think that they have a good handle on addiction urges. However, they miscalculate the cravings that they’ll have when they return to the real world. They tend to fall into many of the same traps that led to the development of addiction. In aftercare, the objective is to guide them in the right direction so that they don’t relapse. Studies suggest that over 2 million people in the United States have a substance use disorder (SUD). Because of that, just over 9 percent of the population uses drugs. Unfortunately, not all of them get the treatment that they need. Making matters worse, people who struggle with SUD are more likely to relapse after treatment. In fact, experts suggest that around 50 percent of them do. Experts believe that it most often occurs during the first few weeks of recovery. If that’s the case, then aftercare could prevent many of these relapses. Of course, a lot of people blow off continued treatment after they finish rehab. As a result, these programs never get to work their magic to prevent relapse.
