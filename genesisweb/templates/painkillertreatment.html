    WHAT TO EXPECT DURING TREATMENT

    Every rehab center is a bit different, but most offer staple programs and services like Detox, Addiction Therapy, and Dual Diagnosis Therapy. Your recovery needs can be determined upon arrival before any painkiller addiction treatment is administered. Depending upon your history with addiction, you might need to undergo detox prior to beginning a painkiller addiction treatment plan. While leftover toxins filter out of your body, your health and symptoms can be closely monitored by caring specialists. Most people also find that their mind is clearer after detox, further benefiting the recovery process. Addiction therapy can be a very important part of your rehab experience, especially if you’ve used painkiller abuse as a coping method in the past. If you expect to get clean and remain sober after treatment, you must put an end to abusive behaviors and adopt healthier habits. Otherwise, you’ll likely return to substance abuse once you return to your real life. Addiction therapy can also help you identify your addiction triggers and any past problems or traumas that you may be running from. From there, you can begin to heal yourself, emotionally and physically. Dual diagnosis treatment could be a part of your treatment plan if you also suffer from a co-occurring emotional condition, like bipolar disorder or schizophrenia. This type of therapy treats the symptoms of addiction and mental illness at the same time. By treating both problems instead of focusing on the addiction, individuals gain a significantly increased chance for total rehabilitation.
