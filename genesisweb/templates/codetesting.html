    Residential Detox Center in Githunguri

    If you think you’re stuck in an unending cycle of “just this one time, and I’ll be done” as the days, months and years go by, it may be time to look for professional help. A detox treatment program is one of the best ways to begin your journey to recovery.

    Detox refers to the process of removing toxins from the body. In the case of drug and alcohol abuse, detox is where the body rids itself of the toxins from the drugs and alcohol.

    There are both inpatient/residential and outpatient detox programs. But someone who has been addicted to drugs and alcohol for a longer period will likely do better in an inpatient detox program.

    A professional will also likely recommend residential detox to those who’ve tried to quit alcohol and substance abuse in vain. This is because there is a higher level of support offered in residential detox treatment programs.

    Outpatient detox, on the other hand, is recommended for those who have used drugs or alcohol for a relatively short period of time and are in generally good health. Outpatient detox works best when there’s at least one person helping you through the recovery process.

    Clients usually stay in an inpatient detox center for as little as four days and as many as two weeks, depending on their individual state.

    Benefits of Drug and Alcohol Detox

    The main goal of detox is to safely alleviate the symptoms of withdrawal. Residential/inpatient detox is more beneficial because:

    • Inpatient detox programs have dedicated staff who are available throughout the day and are well equipped to deal with any side effects of withdrawal.
    • Inpatient detox programs provide a safe haven for recovery far away from temptation. Medications are offered to help with severe withdrawal symptoms.
    • Inpatient programs also offer a home away from home, in that there are no stress factors that may drive one to abuse alcohol and drugs. There are no work stresses, no feuds with family members and no sick kids. Removing stressors helps people focus on their healing process only.
    • Inpatient detox programs are also best for those who may have relapsed after outpatient detox treatment and are looking for a better solution to recovery.

    After Detox

    Detox is not a one-and-done solution to addiction. After detox, it’s important to stay away from people and places where you used to do drugs. Staying away from temptations and old habits can help you develop newer and healthier habits that help you remain drug-free.

    Addiction usually affects the reward center of your brain, leading to dependency. Detox helps you achieve a balance, but it’s important to find psychological stability as well. After detox, the best thing you can do is remain drug-free by entering a drug rehab program that includes individual and group therapy to help you learn to address problems as they arise instead of turning to alcohol and drugs.

    A study conducted at John Hopkins found that individuals who committed to treatment after detox were 10 times more likely to remain substance-free than those who left detox with minimal assistance thereafter. Ready to take that bold step?
