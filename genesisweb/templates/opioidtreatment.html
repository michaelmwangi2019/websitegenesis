    Opioid Addicction Treatment

    Despite the ways that opioid medications help millions of Americans every day, these drugs have a high degree of abuse potential. Add in illicit opioids, such as heroin, and it’s no surprise the U.S. has an opioid crisis sweeping the country. If you or a loved one has been gripped by opioid dependency, it’s time to get opioid addiction treatment. Otherwise, your addiction will only grow worse, leading to consequences that can affect your health, relationships and career.


    THE DANGERS OF OPIOID ADDICTION

    Many people are aware of how dangerous an illicit drug like heroin is. However, they may not give much thought to legal drugs they have in their own homes. Prescription painkillers are among the most widely abused drugs. It’s easy for some people to downplay the abuse potential in some medications because their doctor prescribed them. However, these prescription drugs can be habit-forming and addictive. As users grow use to the euphoric feelings that opioid medications like Vicodin and OxyContin produce, they often develop a tolerance. Over time, they take more of the drugs to feel the same effects. If their doctor stops prescribing the medication, they may turn to “doctor shopping,” purchasing pills from illicit sources, or even turning to other opioids, such as heroin. Without opioid addiction treatment, users run the risk of worsening their physical and mental health.

    COMPREHENSIVE OPIOID ADDICTION TREATMENT

    An opioid addiction treatment center California residents need may provide medication-assisted detox. Methadone and suboxone are two of the most popular options for weaning clients off of opioids. This type of detox is less painful for users and may lead to lower relapse rates since withdrawal won’t be as severe and debilitating. Once someone completes detox, complete opioid addiction treatment will involve therapeutic services designed to integrate all facets of a client’s health. This may include psychotherapy, individual and group therapy. Holistic therapy may also be included to address the spiritual component that’s so important to full recovery.
